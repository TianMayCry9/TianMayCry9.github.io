Single-cell and spatial transcriptomics are revealing the complexity of cellular systems at unprecedented resolution, while comparative genomics is reshaping how we understand the evolutionary forces that shaped them. Yet extracting meaningful biological insight from large-scale multi-omics data remains a fundamental challenge. My work sits at the intersection of algorithm development and data analysis, using computational approaches to study brain evolution and cellular regulation.

## Current Directions

### Comparative Brain Evolution and Developmental Transcriptomics

I apply large-scale computational strategies to integrate single-cell and spatial transcriptomic data across species, tracing the evolutionary trajectories of brain cell types and identifying conserved developmental mechanisms.

### AI for Single-Cell and Spatial Transcriptomics

I develop AI-based computational tools — spanning large language models and deep learning methods — for cell type annotation, cross-species single-cell data integration, and gene expression prediction. I also build AI agent frameworks to automate complex transcriptomic analysis workflows.

### Computational Tools for Evolutionary Biology and Comparative Genomics

I develop computational tools for evolutionary biology and comparative genomics, integrating genomic and phenotypic data to systematically dissect the relationship between genotype and phenotype.
